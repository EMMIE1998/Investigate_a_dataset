Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

The No-show appointment dataset is a collection of information from 110,527 medical appointments in Brazil. Its major focus is to know whether or not patients show up for their appointment. In this analysis we seek to understand which factors are important for us to predict if a patient will show up for the their scheduled appointment.

Below are the factors represented in the columns of the dataset;

1.Appointment ID- this is the patient's ID number.
2.Gender- either male or female.
3.Scheduled Day- the day the patient set up their appointment.
4.Appointment Day- the day of the appointment.
5.Age- how old the patient is.
6.Neighbourhood- indicates the location of the hospital.
7.Scholarship- indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in brasillian welfare program Bosai Familia.
8.Diseases - either they suffer from Hypertention, Diabetes, Alcoholism or are handcap.
9.SMS-Received- indicated whether or not they received reminder SMS about their appointment.
10.No-show- whether or not they showed up for the appointment.

Conclusions¶

After the above analysis these are my findings;

  1. The mean age of the patients that attend their appointments is 37.7 and the patients that do not attend the appointment is 34.3. A difference of 3.4 years. This means that the older people are more likely to attend their appoinmrnts compared to the young.
  2. There are 81 unique hospital locations. From the evaluation we can note that Resistencia and Centro have the highest number of patients who did not show up for their appointment, while Aeroporto and Parque Industrial have the least number of patients that did not attend their appointment.
  3. The patients who had the Brazilian Scholarship are most likely to attend their appointment compared to those that do not have tha Brazilian scholarship.
  4. Female patients have a higher tendancy of attending their appointments compared to the male patients.
  5. Making comparison between gender and scholarship, you find that women with sholarship have a higher tendancy of attending their appointments compared to men with the scholarship.

Limitation¶

Throughout the analysis, I did not encounter major challenges, since the data was pretty clean. It did not have any missing values and it was big enough having 110527 rows to give a good judgement on the questions mentioned above.


However; there was these challenges,

  1. The Neighbourhood column did not indicate which location was closer to the hospital compared to the other.A column of distance from the hospital could have been helpful.
  2. The Handcap entry had more than two entries, that is '0','1' and '2' compared to all the other health conditons that only had two entries that is '0' and '1'.
